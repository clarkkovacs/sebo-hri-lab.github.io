Regular Meetings


Daily Check Ins

Every day at 1:30pm CST we have a brief check in meeting to discuss what each of us been up to for the past 24 hours and what we will be working on next. The purpose of this meeting is to:

  • Keep everyone updated on each other's progress
  • Get input and feedback from one another about what we've been working on
  • Get help from one another if any of us are stuck
  • Provide a chance to socially connect on a regular basis
This is the Zoom link for the daily check ins: https://uchicago.zoom.us/j/95154022010?pwd=SDFHejVjMHV6ZDZxZERQQStudWQxdz09

Weekly HRI Reading Group

Every Wednesday at 4:00pm CST we will discuss one human-robot interaction research paper that Sarah selects. One member of the group (this will rotate) will create a brief presentation summarizing the paper with some discussion questions on the paper. We will discuss the paper as a group. These meetings will help us stay up-to-date on the most recent work in the field of HRI and provide a valuable learning environment to educate ourselves on the cutting-edge research methods and approaches.

This is the Zoom link for the HRI Reading Group: https://uchicago.zoom.us/j/95191860122?pwd=VlczMW9QYm9Odmh1cG83T3d5MVd4Zz09

Here is a suggested format for the presentations for each week:

  • Slide 1: Title of the paper, authors, description image from the paper
  • Slide 2: Summary of the methods of the paper
  • Slide 3: Summary of the main results of the paper
  • Slide 4: Strengths and weaknesses of the paper
  • Slide 5: 5(ish) Discussion Questions

Getting Started

Welcome to the Human-Robot Interaction Lab for summer 2021! Here are some helpful resources to help you get started here in the lab:

  1. Take a look at the For New Lab Members page and follow its instructions to:
    • Join the HRI Lab Slack (if you haven't done so already)
    • Add yourself to the HRI Lab website
    • Send a Slack message to Sarah with your Github username so she can add you to the Github organization
    • Complete the CITI Human Subjects Protection training and email Sarah your certificate (during this internship you will be interacting with human participant data, which is why you must complete this training)
  2. If you will be accessing the lab in-person, please familiarize yourself with the Robotics Lab Access page to get to know the policies and procedures for accessing the HRI lab.
  3. Do some background reading and learning about the projects that you'll be working on. I (Sarah) have listed resources for each project where you can find relevant readings / information to help you get started.
    • Detecting Social Dynamics in Real Time from Prior Dataset (Jason, Efraim)
      • Reading my prior 2 papers (HRI 2020, Frontiers in Psychology 2020) that describe studies and analyses on this dataset is the best place to start. I'd also recommend reading a recent review I wrote on robots that interact with groups and teams.
      • You can find a description of the dataset in this Google Slides that I prepared to explain the dataset to a collaborator of mine.
      • Here's a Google Drive folder with example data from 1 group within this dataset. I'd recommend watching the videos to get a better sense of what these interactions were like.
      • We might find it useful to leverage Microsoft's Platform for Situated Intelligence, so this would be worth checking out.
    • Creating a Tic-Tac-Toe Demo for the Franka Emika Panda Robot (Jason, Efraim)
      • github.com/SeboLab/franka-emika (no need to do the "setup" steps because they've already been done)
      • This Google Doc which contains some helpful password and debugging information.
      • The first task with the Franka Emika Panda robot will be getting MoveIt! successfully installed on the computer controlling the panda so we can use moveit instead of the hiro_ros_arm_controller.
      • The Azure Kinect will be the "eyes" of the robot, here's a helpful setup guide which might be useful in getting the kinect up and running.
    • Exploring How Robots May Enhance Human-Human Conversations (Stephanie, Javier)
      • This Google Drive folder contains all of the relevant project-related information. Please specifically examine the "Getting Started - Stephanie & Javier" folder for some background reading and information.
    • Sim2Real for Intro Robotics CMSC 20600 (Stephanie, Jason)
      • The Turtlebot3 online manual (also includes assembly manuals)
      • The OpenManipulator online manual (also includes assembly manuals)
      • The Intro Robo Spring 2021 Github organization
      • The Intro Robo Spring 2022 Github organization (both of you should have been invited to be members of this organization)


Expectations


Work Hours

You are more than welcome to work whatever hours you like as long as:

  1. You are working 37.5 hours per week (this is UChicago's maximum number of hours a student can work during a week)
  2. You are working during the core work hours of 10am - 4pm (this may change depending on everyone's working hour preferences) where it's reasonable to assume that you will take a lunch break of 15 min - 1 hour somewhere in that time frame
  3. You attend all group meetings.
